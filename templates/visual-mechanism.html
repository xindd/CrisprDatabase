(a). AcrF1 interacts with Cas7f, sterically hindering target DNA access to the crRNA guide.

(b). AcrF2 interacts with Cas8f and Cas7f, resembling a DNA duplex and sterically hindering target dsDNA access to the binding pocket.

(c). AcrF3 forms a homodimer, interacting with Cas3 and impeding the recruitment of Cas3 to Cascade.

(d). AcrF10 acts as a DNA mimic, binding to residues vital for target DNA binding, and sterically hindering target DNA access to the binding pocket.

(e). AcrIIA4 occupies the PAM-interacting site, and interacting with the surrounding basic residues vital for PAM recognition.

(f). AcrIIA2 interacts with SpyCas9 in a sgRNA-dependent manner, hinderng target DNA binding.

(g). AcrIIC1 interacts with the HNHNme domain, shielding the HNH catalytic domain and inhibiting the DNA cleavage activity.

(h). AcrIIC3 blocks activity of a single Cas9 ortholog and induces Cas9 dimerization while preventing binding to the target DNA.
